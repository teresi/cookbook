\begin{recipeabstract}
    Pork shoulder braised and chopped.
    Bayless provides a few methods to recreate the dish in lieu of giant copper cauldron full of lard.
    The slow cooker method is adapted here, which allows for some spice as frying in lard would otherwise `obliterate' it.
    Spices are usually either just salt and pepper or cinnamon, citrus, oregano, and the like.
    Martinez recommends Canela cinnamon which has a sweeter flavor than Cassia.
    People often include ingredients to encourage browning such as cola, citrus, condensed milk, or sugar.
\end{recipeabstract}

\begin{preparation}
\item Reserve a large dutch oven \& iron skillet, preheat to $250$ $\Fahrenheit$.
\item Debone pork butt, cut into large chunks, salt, set aside.
\item Melt sugar in the stock.
\item Cut garlic head in half, add to cheesecloth with oregano, peppercorns, chili flakes.
\item Cut the onion, cut oranges in half.
\ragmarpar{Don't splash the oil!}
\item Melt lard on medium, brown pork, deglaze w/ stock.
\item Add pork, onion, beer, sachet.
\item Squeeze oranges, add juice to stock, add one orange peel.
\item Roast covered $\approx5$ $\hour$, until it reaches 207 $\Fahrenheit$.
\item Lower to $225$ $\Fahrenheit$, roast $\ge$ 30 $\minute$ or until ready to eat.
\item Remove the peel, sachet, canela, bay leaf.
\item Transfer pieces to a cutting board, chop.
\item Broil or fry the pork in the skillet with some of the juice until it browns, serve.
\end{preparation}
